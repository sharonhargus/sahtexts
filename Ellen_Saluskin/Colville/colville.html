This is, today is June the 3rd, and Ellen Saluskin is going to make a tape
here, relating some of her experiences with her husband Alex Saluskin,
who was half Wiinátshapam through his mother, Louisa Saluskin,
Tímantwaa. She was a Tímantwaa, and she was from the Topey family
tree, I understand. I'm not really familiar with all of this. But I'm
just putting this on the tape so that we'll understand what Ellen
Saluskin is going to talk about. And she was married to him for about
50 years. And she travelled with him to various political activities
involving his people up north, and she travelled with him when they
did the boundary claims survey with the tribal attorney, Paul Niebel,
from Washington D.C. She was their cook and she provided the
transportation for the group. And just her and Thomas Yallup and Alex
Saluskin and Paul Niebel travelled around establishing the northern
boundary of the Yakama Nation treaty claim that involved the
Wiinátshapam tribe, and all the other, Chelan and Entiat tribes. So
she's going to tape something about Coulee Dam, when President Hoover
[actually it was Harry Truman] dedicated that dam up there. And she's going to tape something about
that today.
